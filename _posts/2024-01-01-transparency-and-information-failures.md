The pattern held across two years: residents and council members asked for information and did not get it.

- **Late or missing FOIL responses.** Multiple residents and council members reported FOIL requests being denied or ignored by the city attorney.
- **Last-minute agenda postings.** Speakers at the January 8, 2026 public forum described agendas posted hours before votes and materials reaching the council the same day as consequential decisions.
- **No redline versions of policy changes.** The December 18, 2025 Finance Meeting revealed the administration had no redline version of a five-year update to the non-represented employee policy. Council members could not tell what had changed.
- **No response to community proposals.** At the January 8, 2026 public forum, resident Drea Leanza described submitting a proposal to build owner-occupied homes on a city-owned South Troy lot, with TAP and Habitat for Humanity involved, nearly two years earlier with no response from the administration. Resident Natalie Jabau described a rezoning correction letter sent in October 2025 to the council and mayor, also with no response.

**The FOIL system on the record**

At the April 23, 2026 law committee meeting, Communications Director Alex Horton, the city's designated FOIL officer, testified that his training consisted of a single in-house walkthrough of the GovQA software system. He could not recall the name of the staffer who conducted it. He estimated spending one to two hours per day on FOIL work. He had never provided an annual FOIL compliance report to the council.

Corporation Counsel Morrissey, who could not recall the last time he received FOIL training of any kind, confirmed that he is the person who trained Horton on which exemptions apply to which requests. Morrissey also told the committee that municipalities are not legally bound by opinions of the New York State Committee on Open Government, the state body that advises municipalities on FOIL compliance. At the same meeting, CSEA union representatives testified they had been unable to reach Morrissey for months on basic labor relations matters.

When residents appeal a FOIL denial, the appeal goes to Morrissey. There is no one else to appeal to. At a January 22, 2026 Public Safety Committee meeting, Morrissey acknowledged he had fallen behind on appeals during an illness.

*Source: Troy City Council Jan. 8, 2026 meeting transcript; Dec. 18, 2025 meeting transcript (City Clerk's office); Troy City Council Law Committee and Finance Meeting, April 23, 2026 (transcript); Troy City Council Public Safety Committee, January 22, 2026 (transcript)*
