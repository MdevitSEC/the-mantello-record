Twenty-two people showed up to speak at the new council's first public forum on January 8, 2026. Mantello was not there. Neither was anyone from her administration. Speakers noticed.

Sean Collins of the Troy Area Labor Council, AFL-CIO, said it plainly from the podium: "The mayor's absence at the new council's first public meeting is indicative of what the next two years of your term will be."

Council President Steele acknowledged the absence in her closing remarks.

The meeting had been converted to a standalone public forum because no legislative items were ready. All seven council members were present. They took notes and said they would follow up on concerns raised.

Speakers covered ground that had been building for two years: 18 months without quarterly financial reports, budgets submitted minutes before deadlines, agendas posted hours before votes, FOIL requests ignored, community proposals sent to the administration with no reply. The Frear Park pavilion bond passed December 30 with no plans on file. The sustainability task force had accumulated $837,000 in available NYSERDA grants the administration had refused to access. Downtown benches had been removed with no documentation. The sound ordinance had been enforced without a council vote.

The meeting had no executive presence. The 22 speakers had seven minutes each and no dialogue with the people who run the city.

*Source: Troy City Council Regular Meeting, January 8, 2026 (transcript; no formal minutes filed)*
