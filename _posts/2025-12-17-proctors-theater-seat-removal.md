Photos hit social media showing every orchestra-level seat pulled out of the former Proctor's Theater, the building Mantello was converting into city hall. The images landed awkwardly against earlier administration assurances about preserving the theater's historic character. A city communications director said the seats would go back in and floated an "adopt a seat" program.

What no one disputed: the project carries no legal obligation to preserve anything, because it does not use historic preservation tax credits.

**The preservation claim, on the record**

At the November 6, 2025 Finance Meeting, where the council debated and passed the 30-year lease, Deputy Mayor Donnelly said the State Historic Preservation Office supports the project as an adaptive reuse model and that a SHPO letter had been shared with the council. Council President Steele disputed this characterization directly on the floor: the letter does not commend the project and does not describe it as a model. She also noted that because the LDC is not applying for historic tax credits, SHPO has no enforcement role regardless. The administration's preservation claims were not backed by any legal mechanism.

At the same meeting, Corporation Counsel Morrissey acknowledged that the lease contract was still a draft when the council voted on it.
