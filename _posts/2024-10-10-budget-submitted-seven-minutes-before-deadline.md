Mantello filed the 2025 budget seven minutes before the legal deadline. Corrected materials arrived almost an hour after it. Council President Steele, along with Council Members Vera and Spain-McLaren, said the timing "falls far short of the level of transparency that taxpayers deserve." The Republican majority passed the budget anyway, 4-3 along party lines.

The city had no permanent comptroller from July 2024 through the entire budget season, leaning on outside firms that had already cost more than $260,000.

**The accounting crisis behind the budget**

At the October 15, 2024 special budget meeting, representatives from BST, the accounting firm the city brought in to reconstruct its finances, laid out why the budget existed at all. The problem started with KVS, the city's financial software, which BST representatives compared to Windows 95. The system runs as two separate systems: one where transactions are entered and a second where those transactions must be manually processed. When that processing runs, all users must log out and transactions must hold. Because the process is manual and sequential, entries regularly landed in wrong accounts or wrong funds. The prior comptroller had managed this by maintaining clearing accounts and doing manual reclassifications. When he left, no one else knew how.

BST found more than 60 journal entries it had to develop itself as part of the 2023 reconciliation, some involving multi-million dollar adjustments. The administration told the council a new accounting software proposal would arrive within one to six weeks. Implementation would take 12 to 18 months.

**The numbers**

The 2025 proposed budget was approximately $5 million larger than 2024, with every dollar of the increase coming from fixed mandated costs: pensions, health insurance, and debt service, which rose $1.1 million alone. The administration held the tax rate increase to 1.89 percent, which it described as the lowest in recent years, and came in $89,415 under the tax cap.

The garbage fee increased from $162, set as a temporary fee in 2018, to $252. The mayor called the increase necessary to compete with private haulers and acknowledged it was now a permanent fee.

**The delivery**

The budget arrived electronically near midnight on October 15. The document was not searchable and had no index. A hard copy did not arrive until the afternoon of the October 15 meeting. Council President Steele put her frustration on the record: "I know for a fact that if you were sitting in this seat you would be just as frustrated as I am." Mayor Mantello defended the timeline, calling the circumstances extraordinary.

Three comptroller candidates were in the pipeline at the time. All seven council members voted to accept receipt of the budget.
