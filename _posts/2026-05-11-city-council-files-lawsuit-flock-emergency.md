Troy's City Council and City Auditor took Mantello to Rensselaer County Supreme Court in May, asking a judge to declare her April 1 emergency order unlawful, nullify it, and throw out the Flock Safety contract renewal. The legal complaint is direct: Mantello used emergency powers to authorize a $78,000 payment without council or auditor approval, cutting them out of the process entirely.

Council President Steele said the declaration "overstepped legislative authority."

**The record leading to the lawsuit**

At the April 23, 2026 law committee meeting, the unscheduled public forum that followed was focused entirely on the Flock emergency. The meeting fell on day 23 of the state of emergency. Francis Sweet of South Troy cited the full list of emergency declarations Mantello had issued since taking office: the Flock declaration was her tenth.

Sylva Maynard reported a recent development in Oshkosh, Wisconsin: the Oshkosh City Council voted 7-0 to rescind approval of its Flock contract after the police chief discovered that Flock had provided false information to the council about its data practices. The chief informed the city manager the following morning; within one day the council held a special session, rescinded the contract, and apologized to the community. Maynard said she had transcripts of both Oshkosh meetings available.

Bex Cahill pointed to Troy City Charter Section C-40, which defines an emergency as conflagration, riot, storm, earthquake, or other unusual peril to life and property. She argued that continuation of a vendor contract does not meet that standard.
