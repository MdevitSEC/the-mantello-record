Moving city hall to Proctor's created two immediate problems at the old location. The city owed its current landlord, First Columbia, $226,807 in Common Area Maintenance fees from 2024. Mantello disputed the CAM math but acknowledged the bill was unpaid. More seriously, First Columbia's attorneys argued the city had already missed the deadline to exercise its early-exit option, meaning Troy could be trapped in its River Street lease through January 2029 — two years past the planned move date.

**The dispute's origins**

The city had signed a new lease with First Columbia in July 2024, backdated to January 1, 2024. The new lease included CAM charges approximately four times higher than before. The city paid rent throughout 2024 but refused to pay the elevated CAM charges, arguing proper notice of the increase had not been given under the lease's terms.

Two separate notices of claim followed: one for the alleged lease termination and one for the unpaid CAM charges. At the November 20, 2025 Finance Meeting, resident Noreen McKee asked for clarification on the total exposure — $1.4 million in notices of claims related to delinquent CAM charges and rent at 433 River Street. The council did not provide a full answer from the floor.

**The settlement**

Corporation Counsel Morrissey announced a settlement that same night: the city would pay $134,000 in 2024 CAM charges, the full disputed amount, without interest or penalty. In exchange, the city would support First Columbia's application to the Troy IDA for a 10-year extension of the building's PILOT agreement. If the IDA did not approve the amendment, both parties reserved the right to return to litigation.

Morrissey framed it as a near-win: the building is a significant piece of the downtown streetscape and the city is vacating it regardless, so supporting an IDA application costs nothing. Council Member Brosnan was not satisfied: "If there were no dispute, there'd be no need to settle the claim." He noted the council had approved the July 2024 lease that created the very CAM charge dispute now being settled.

Council President Steele abstained, citing her membership on the IDA board. Council Member Vera voted no. The settlement passed 5-1-1.
