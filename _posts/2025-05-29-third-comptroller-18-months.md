Michael McNeff was Troy's third comptroller in roughly 18 months. Before him came Jack Krokos, hired in January 2025. Before Krokos came Dylan Spring, hired in February 2024 and gone by July. Before all of them came a vacancy: Mantello started her term in January 2024 without a comptroller at all, the first time that had happened in 40 years. The revolving door cost the city more than $260,000 in outside accounting fees.

In the meantime, the city's finances ran through Gabrielle Mahoney, a city employee whose permanent title was Chief Account Clerk. During the budget season, the administration introduced her to the council as "City Treasurer/Chief Account Clerk." By May 2025, she had been confirmed as temporary treasurer three times. Corporation Counsel Morrissey said no formal hearing was required because "confirmation of an appointment to the office of treasurer is not required under our charter."

The reason for the third confirmation was not charter compliance. The Environmental Facilities Corporation was withholding grant funds owed to Troy, including money for lead waterline replacement, because EFC's legal counsel wanted documentation that the city's chief fiscal officer was formally authorized. Morrissey called it a "comfort confirmation." He told the council the city's charter makes the treasurer the deputy controller, and that documentation was needed only because EFC asked for it.

Before the vote, Council Member Vera asked for a resume. Morrissey said he didn't have one. His explanation: "I don't know if there is a resume at the present time." Mahoney was in the hallway outside the chambers during that exchange. A council member asked if she could come in and speak for herself. The answer was no.

The council voted 7-0 to confirm her.
