Michael McNeff was Troy's third comptroller in roughly 18 months. Before him came Jack Krokos, hired in January 2025 and publicly welcomed at the January 23 Finance Meeting. Before Krokos came Dylan Spring, hired in February 2024 and gone by July. Before all of them came a vacancy: Mantello started her term in January 2024 without a comptroller at all, the first time that had happened in 40 years. The revolving door cost the city more than $260,000 in outside accounting fees.

The comptroller's office was not the only vacancy. By May 2025, the city also had no permanent DPU commissioner and no director of operations at the water plant. The water plant was running on a consulting contract with DCK Services, whose principal held the licenses required to operate it legally under state DOH and DEC requirements. A director of operations position had been posted at a budgeted salary of roughly $95,000, but no one had been hired.

**The treasurer who wasn't supposed to need confirming**

In the meantime, the city's finances ran through Gabrielle Mahoney, a city employee of roughly a dozen years whose permanent title was Chief Account Clerk. During the budget season, the administration introduced her to the council as "City Treasurer/Chief Account Clerk." By May 2025, she had been confirmed as temporary treasurer three times.

Corporation Counsel Morrissey said no formal hearing was required because confirmation of the treasurer was not required under the city charter. The reason the confirmation went forward anyway had nothing to do with the charter. The Environmental Facilities Corporation was withholding grant funds owed to Troy, including money for lead waterline replacement, because EFC's legal counsel wanted documentation that the city's chief fiscal officer was formally authorized. Morrissey called it a "comfort confirmation."

Before the vote, Council Member Vera asked for a resume. Morrissey said he didn't have one. His explanation: "I don't know if there is a resume at the present time." Vera was direct: "I'm very disappointed that we're unable to provide a resume if indeed one exists." Mahoney was in the hallway outside the chambers during that exchange. A council member asked if she could come in and speak for herself. The answer was no.

The council voted 7-0 to confirm her.
