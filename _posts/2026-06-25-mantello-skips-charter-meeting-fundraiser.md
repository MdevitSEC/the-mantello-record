The Troy City Council called a special meeting on June 25, 2026, to override Mayor Mantello's veto of a resolution scheduling a public hearing on Local Law 4. The meeting lasted 12 minutes. Mantello was not there. She had a fundraiser.

The Troy City Charter requires the mayor to attend all regular and special meetings of the City Council and to take part in discussion. She did not.

---

## What Local Law 4 Would Do

Local Law 4 is a charter amendment that would redefine "disaster" to a specific list of qualifying events: fire, flood, earthquake, hurricane, tornado, high water, landslide, wind, storm, wave action, epidemic, air contamination, terrorism, cyber event, blight, drought, infestation, explosion, water contamination, and bridge failure or collapse. Under the amended charter, Mantello could not declare a public emergency unless the situation fell within one of those categories.

The amendment is a direct response to her April 1, 2026 emergency declaration over Flock Safety license plate reader cameras. She had used emergency powers to authorize a $78,000 payment without council or auditor approval after the council let the contract renewal deadline lapse while debating privacy protections. Council President Steele put her objection plainly: "That's why we're amending the charter. It's open-ended with her."

---

## The Veto of the Hearing

A public hearing is a required procedural step before a local law amending the city charter can be adopted. The council scheduled that hearing. Mantello vetoed the resolution scheduling it, blocking the process before it could begin.

That is what the June 25 special meeting was called to address. The council overrode the veto in 12 minutes.

---

## The Fundraiser

The City Charter requires the mayor to attend all regular and special meetings of the City Council. Mantello chose not to attend. She had a fundraiser scheduled for the same day.

This is not the first time. At the new council's first public forum on January 8, 2026, Mantello was absent along with her entire administration. Twenty-two residents showed up to speak. Sean Collins of the Troy Area Labor Council said from the podium: "The mayor's absence at the new council's first public meeting is indicative of what the next two years of your term will be."

The June 25 meeting was not a public forum. It was a formal special meeting of the City Council on a specific action she had taken. The charter language is not ambiguous. She was required to be there.

---

**Partisan angle:** Mantello used emergency powers to bypass the council and the city auditor on a contract dispute. The council is amending the charter to make sure she cannot do it again. She vetoed even the scheduling of the required public hearing. The council overrode her in 12 minutes while she was at a fundraiser. She skipped a meeting she was legally required to attend to raise campaign money.
